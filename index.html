My landlord won't return my security deposit after I vacated +
✅ Court-suitable
This is one of the most common and winnable cases in India. You can approach the Consumer Forum or file a civil suit under the Transfer of Property Act. If your deposit is under ₹50 lakh, the Consumer Forum is faster and free. You need your rent agreement, deposit receipt, and vacating notice. The average resolution time via Consumer Forum is 3–6 months. NALSA can provide free legal aid if you cannot afford a lawyer.
My employer hasn't paid my salary for 3+ months +
✅ Court-suitable
File a complaint with the Labour Commissioner under the Payment of Wages Act, 1936. This is free, fast (30–90 days), and you can also claim compensation. You need your appointment letter, salary slips for previous months, and bank statements. If the employer threatens you, that itself is an offence under the Industrial Disputes Act.
I bought a defective product and the company is refusing a refund +
✅ Consumer Forum
File at the District Consumer Disputes Redressal Forum. For claims under ₹50 lakh, there is no court fee. Keep your invoice, warranty card, and all communication with the company. The Consumer Protection Act 2019 entitles you to a replacement, refund, or compensation. Cases typically resolve in 90–180 days.
Government hasn't started my father's pension after retirement +
⚠️ Try grievance portal first
First file on the Centralized Public Grievance Redress and Monitoring System (CPGRAMS) at pgportal.gov.in — this often resolves within 30 days at no cost. If unresolved, file a writ petition in the High Court. Pension delay cases have a very high success rate and courts often award interest on delayed payments.
